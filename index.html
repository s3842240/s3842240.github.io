Description

The world is on fire; entire continents are gone, swallowed by the depths of the ocean as the nuclear fallout of every nation comes down. A struggle for military dominance, naval and air superiority ensues, and military bases of all nations are taken out by others. No one comes out on top and most nations are engulfed in toxic pollution and radiation following the detonation of multiple nuclear devices and bombs within their cities and towns. Billions perished within the first few hours of the global catastrophe and more died due to exposure to the deadly radiation and pollution. Surprisingly only the Eastern side of Australia was hit by nuclear devices and in the Western side only the military bases were taken out by other nations. Small island nations and parts of continents survived but struggle to cope in the damaged environment that has mostly turned into water.

To continue to survive, the remaining military of Australia worked together to create a ship out of the scrap and rubble left behind in the wreckages once called cities. They created a ship that used to sail around in the days of pirates and Kings, with a few technological upgrades. The protagonist leads a small team of soldiers and naval workers on a surveying voyage to explore what remains of other nations and continents. The idea behind this game is to explore other nations in a series of missions and uncover the truth behind the cause of this global catastrophe. Characters will interact with each other and the protagonist and create bonds, friendships and even relationships. Learning more about the world around you and learning about the people closest to you will provide meaningful insight and story into this post-apocalyptic hellscape, that is the Earth. Innovations of existing technologies will exist, like ATV’s with light-machine guns on the rear for the passenger to use to explore hostile and dangerous lands, jet skis with automatic rifles strapped to either side for exploration in rivers and inland bodies of water. Cool additions to existing technology and bringing pirating and exploring the extended 7 seas into the modern era. As previously mapped out maps of the world will only be vague guided to the new devastated world. Economies crumbled, and currencies became worthless as governments of surviving nations tried to stabilise their lands.

Obviously that scale is unrealistic for a project, but something that is possible is to create a section or one mission. The possibilities are endless in this world I have thought up.
